About Us

Who We Are

Sirowa Sacco is the pillar of Luo community. We offer Luos, at home and abroad, young and old, a financial support mechanism to help them make use of their resources, and achieve financial freedom through savings and through enterprises.

The Problem We Seek To Solve As Sirowa Sacco

The Luo community needs USD. 10bn in financial services over the next 10 years to become competitive and fully address the gap in stimulating the seven (7) strategic sectors of digital credit, movable assets, housing, farming, business, social services, and table banking.

The sector opportunities are as below:

  • 1.3 million+ Luos with digital devices who seek affordable digital credit service;
  • 200,000 vehicles and bodabodas on pay as you go -PAYGO- asset financing;
  • 400,000 households seeking affordable housing in urban centres
  • 1 million+ acres of unused land due to lack of innovative financing
  • 200,000+ businesses seeking innovative business financing to scale their businesses
  • 5,000+ table banking organisations seeking financing and entrepreneurship training
  • 200+ social service institutions, schools, hospitals, hospices, polytechnics, and universities

Frequently Asked Questions

    1. Have fully paid registration fees (KES. 1,200 for individuals and KES. 3,600 for groups/corporates/CBOs).
    2. Have fully paid shareholding (KES.5,000 for individuals and KES. 25,000 corporates, groups, CBOs)
    3. Have matching saving to the loan, 15% for individual loans and 10% for group loans.
    4. Have collateral in form of security or guarantor savings.

    1. 12% p.a. interest rates for asset financing loans including buildings and land.
    2. 24% p.a. interest rates for movable assets including motorcycles, motor vehicles, and machinery.
    3. Individuals can get upto 7X of their savings groups upto 10X of their savings.
    4. Digital loans at 7% monthly.
    5. Emergency loans to non members at 15% monthly interest, secured by motor vehicles.

    1. Dividends from Shares- members buy shares, and earn profits, called dividends, every year.
    2. Interest income from savings - members save, and earn profit called interest, every end of month, end of quarter, end of six months, or end of year.
    3. Income from joint projects- members can form groups with fellow members, and do special projects, and share in profits, like diary project, sisal planting, chicken project, etc.
    4. Welfare- members benefit from group welfare products like financial education sessions, group insurance at cheaper rates
    5. Loans- members access loans at low interest rates, and as much as 10 times their savings for groups and 7 times savings for individuals, secured by collateral or savings of the member or other members (guarantees).
    6. Community Development- members, through their deposits, lead to luo community development by allowing their funds to be borrowed by other members of the community to support business projects.
